🌾 Cooperative Structure — Important Context

Banrural is a cooperative development bank with a unique ownership structure: owned by cooperatives, municipalities, NGOs, and rural organizations. Decision-making involves multiple stakeholders and may take longer than traditional banks. Focus on social impact and rural development alongside profitability.

🌾 Cooperative Context — Why It Matters

Banrural's cooperative structure fundamentally shapes how they make decisions:

  • Mission-driven: Financial inclusion > pure profit maximization
  • Stakeholder governance: Cooperatives, municipalities, NGOs on the board
  • Rural focus: 1,000+ branches, many in remote areas
  • Longer decisions: Board approval for major investments
  • Social impact: Technology must serve underserved populations
  • Agent network: 10,000+ agents extending reach beyond branches

💡 Sales tip: Frame your solutions in terms of how they help Banrural serve more rural Guatemalans, not just cost savings.
