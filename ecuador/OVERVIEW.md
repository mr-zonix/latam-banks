# 🇪🇨 ECUADOR — Banking Sector Intelligence

## Regulatory Environment

**Supervisor:** Superintendencia de Bancos del Ecuador (SB)
- Conservative regulatory stance
- Strong capital adequacy requirements
- Increasing pressure on digital banking compliance
- Open Banking regulations: nascent but emerging (no formal framework yet)

**Key Regulatory Dynamics:**
- Dollarized economy (USD) — eliminates FX risk, simplifies infrastructure
- High liquidity requirements post-2008 crisis legacy
- Growing AML/KYC compliance pressure
- Data localization preferences (not mandated, but preferred)

## Market Concentration

The Ecuador banking sector is **highly concentrated**. The top 5 banks control approximately 75% of total assets, making them the only commercially relevant targets for enterprise infrastructure sales.

**Implication:** Focus all efforts on Top 5. The remaining 20+ banks are either too small, too public-sector, or too niche for significant infrastructure deals.

## Infrastructure Investment Drivers

### Pressures Creating Buying Appetite

1. **Digital Competition**
   - Neobanks (Banco del Barrio digital, fintech entrants)
   - Pressure to modernize customer channels

2. **Operational Efficiency**
   - High CIR across sector (~50-60%)
   - Strong business case for automation

3. **Regulatory Compliance**
   - AML/KYC system upgrades
   - Reporting and audit trail requirements

4. **Data Growth**
   - Transaction volumes increasing
   - Storage and analytics infrastructure strain

5. **Core Banking Technical Debt**
   - Several banks running legacy cores (15-20 years old)
   - Modernization or replacement cycles approaching
