🎖️ Unique Positioning: Military & Police Bank

BANCOM is owned by the Caja de Pensiones Militar Policial (CPMP) — the pension fund for Peru's Armed Forces and National Police. This gives them a captive customer base but also limits growth potential. Key clients: military personnel, police officers, retirees, and related government entities.

🏢 Bank Profile

📋 General Information

  • Founded: 1967 (as BANPECO), 2004 (reorganized)
  • Headquarters: Av. Canaval y Moreyra 452, San Isidro, Lima
  • Type: Private Niche Bank
  • License: Full Commercial Bank (SBS)
  • Listed: BVL (BCOMPC1)
  • Rebrand: "BANCOM" since 2023

👥 Ownership & Control

  • Control: Caja de Pensiones Militar Policial (CPMP)
  • Ownership: 99.99% CPMP
  • Structure: Single institutional owner
  • Decision Authority: Board appointed by CPMP
  • Public Float: ~0.01% (minimal)

📍 Scale & Footprint

  • Employees: 726 (2024)
  • Branches: ~35-40 offices
  • ATMs: ~100 (estimated)
  • Market Position: #10-11 in Peru
  • Focus: Military bases, police stations, pension offices

💡 Strategic Position

BANCOM operates as a captive bank for Peru's military and police pension system. While small (~1-2% market share), they have guaranteed revenue from pension payments, salary processing, and loans to armed forces personnel. Low competition risk in their niche, but limited growth potential outside it.

  • Family members

💡 Sales Angle

CPMP controls both the bank AND the pension fund. Infrastructure decisions may be made at CPMP level for both entities. A solution sold to BANCOM could potentially expand to CPMP's pension management systems. Consider positioning for the broader CPMP ecosystem, not just the bank.
