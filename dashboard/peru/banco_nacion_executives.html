👔 Banco de la Nación — Executive Intelligence

🏛️ State-Owned Bank • 100% Government of Peru • Public procurement required

🏛️ GOVERNMENT BANK — Special Sales Process

Banco de la Nación is 100% owned by the Government of Peru. This fundamentally changes how you sell:

  • ALL purchases must go through public tender (licitación pública)
  • No direct sales — you cannot close a deal through executive relationships alone
  • Executives change with government administrations (every 5 years max)
  • Budget is government budget — approved annually by Congress
  • Pitch "citizen service" not ROI — public impact matters more than profit

📋 SEACE — Required Procurement Portal

ALL Banco de la Nación purchases go through SEACE (Sistema Electrónico de Contrataciones del Estado):

  • 🔗 SEACE Portal → gob.pe/seace
  • 🔍 Search for: "Banco de la Nación" to find active tenders
  • 📅 Monitor regularly — tenders have strict deadlines
  • 📝 Register as a provider to participate in tenders
  • 🤝 Local partner often required for foreign companies

Strategy: Build relationships with executives to influence RFP requirements, but the actual sale happens through SEACE tender.

⚠️ Research Status — Political Appointees

Banco de la Nación executives are political appointees that change with government administrations. Always verify current leadership before meetings — turnover is high. Last verified: April 2025.

📋 Public Procurement Guide — How to Win

📅 Timeline

  • Budget planning: Jul-Oct (for next year)
  • Tender publication: Varies (monitor SEACE)
  • Q&A period: 1-2 weeks typically
  • Proposal deadline: Strict — no extensions
  • Contract to award: 3-6 months

✅ How to Win

  • Build relationships BEFORE tender
  • Help shape RFP requirements
  • Register on SEACE early
  • Local partner for foreign companies
  • Competitive pricing (often lowest bid wins)

❌ Common Mistakes

  • Trying to close deals directly
  • Ignoring SEACE registration
  • Missing tender deadlines
  • Incomplete documentation
  • Underestimating local partners

💡 Pro Tips

  • Pitch "citizen impact" not ROI
  • Reference other government clients
  • Financial stability matters (bonds)
  • Spanish documentation required
  • Expect 6-18 month sales cycles
